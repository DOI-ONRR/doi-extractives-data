Join a Resource Advisory Council. BLM formed {{ "Resource Advisory Councils" | term:"resource advisory council (rac)" }} (RACs) in the western U.S. to provide advice on managing public lands and resources. Each council consists of 12 to 15 members with diverse interests in local communities, such as ranchers, environmental groups, tribes, state and local government officials, academics, and other public land users. Join your local Resource Advisory Council.

Volunteer. Find opportunities in your area or create a BLM partnership.

Contact a local BLM office. BLM has 12 state and regional field offices, mostly in the western U.S. Find your state office for details about land-use policies in your area.
